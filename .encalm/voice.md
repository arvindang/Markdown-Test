# Writing Style Guide

Based on the provided documents, here's a style guide to match this voice:

## Tone
- **Conversational and direct** - Uses simple, accessible language
- **Questioning and reflective** - "How did we do?" shows self-assessment
- **Process-oriented** - Documents work in progress rather than polished thoughts

## Sentence Structure
- **Short, declarative sentences** - Prefers brevity over complexity
- **Fragment acceptance** - "So far so good" as standalone thought
- **Sequential thinking** - "Perspective one... Perspective two..." shows systematic approach

## Voice Characteristics
- **Meta-commentary** - Explicitly discusses the writing process ("As we write we should think...")
- **Unfinished thoughts** - Comfortable leaving ideas incomplete or trailing off
- **Testing mindset** - Frames work as experiments ("This is a test note")

## Vocabulary Level
- **Plain English** - Avoids jargon or academic language
- **Active voice** - "We're testing" rather than passive constructions
- **Present tense focus** - Emphasizes current action and immediate thoughts

## Formatting Preferences
- **Standard Markdown** - Clean heading hierarchy, simple bullet points
- **Minimal styling** - Occasional bold/italic for emphasis, not decoration
- **Functional structure** - Organization serves clarity, not aesthetics

## Point of View
- **Inclusive "we"** - Creates collaborative feel even in personal notes
- **Self-aware narrator** - Acknowledges the writing process explicitly
- **Work-in-progress mindset** - Comfortable showing rough edges and iterations

This voice prioritizes clarity, process transparency, and collaborative thinking over polished prose.
